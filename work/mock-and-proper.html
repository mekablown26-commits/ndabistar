The Brief

Mock & Proper needed a brand identity that walked the line between playful irreverence and sharp professionalism. The name itself is a contradiction — and that tension became the creative engine for the whole visual system.

The challenge was to create something that felt premium without being stiff, and edgy without losing credibility. Every decision — from typeface to colour — had to serve that balance.

The Approach

The logo was built around typographic tension — pairing a refined serif-adjacent wordmark with bold, confident spacing. The letterforms needed to feel like they owned the room the moment they walked in.

The banner expanded that identity into a horizontal format — creating a sense of breadth and authority. Colour was kept intentional: a palette that signals quality without shouting it.

The result is a brand that can flex across digital and print — equally at home on a business card or a billboard.
